Green Campus, Better Future

A fresh campus starts with mindful waste handling, smart segregation, and community action.

🌍 Why Waste Management Matters

  • 🌱 Preserves Nature: Keeps our campus green, clean, and free from toxic buildup.
  • ♻️ Saves Resources: Recycling paper and plastic reduces the need for raw materials.
  • 🚫 Prevents Diseases: Proper disposal stops the breeding of mosquitoes and harmful bacteria.
  • 📉 Reduces Emissions: Less rotting waste means fewer greenhouse gases in our air.
  • 🤝 Builds Community: Working together creates a sense of pride and responsibility for our college.

Consequences of Poor Waste Management

Environmental Pollution
Overflowing bins contaminate the soil and release toxic chemicals into the campus groundwater.
Global Warming
Decomposing organic waste in open dumps releases methane, a powerful greenhouse gas.
Health Issues
Accumulated garbage attracts pests, leading to respiratory problems and infectious diseases.
Marine Life
Plastics washed into campus drains eventually reach rivers, choking aquatic life.
Economic Impact
The college spends excess funds on heavy cleanup drives instead of student facilities.

Solutions for a Better Environment

🧺

Waste Segregation

Separate wet and dry waste at the source using color-coded bins.

🌿

Composting at Home

Turn leftover canteen food and dry leaves into nutrient-rich soil.

📦

Smart Recycling

Collect paper, cardboard, and plastic bottles for the local scrap dealer.

💧

Zero Waste Lifestyle

Carry reusable water bottles and cloth bags to minimize daily trash.

🌾

Eco-Friendly Products

Switch to biodegradable pens, bamboo toothbrushes, and paper cups.

🤝

Community Initiatives

Join the weekly BIS group cleanup drives and spread awareness.
